The Russo Japanese War

On 8 February 1904 ships from the Imperial Japanese Navy (I.J.N) launched a surprise attack on The Russian Far East fleet anchored in Port Arthur, this leads to the beginning of the Russo – Japanese war “interestingly enough the Japanese attacked before the official declaration of war was sent a strategy, they would also employ for the Pearl Harbour attack against the Americans”.

The Reinforcements: Formation of the 2nd Pacific Squadron

With the First Pacific Squadron either inactive or scattered and Japan tightening its hold on Port Arthur, The Russians came up with a plan to send reinforcements either from the Black Sea Fleet or the Baltic Fleet. These ships would link up with the 1st Pacific Squadron and overwhelm the Japanese navy. With the situation deteriorating the Tsar agreed to the formation of the second pacific squadron. It would be placed under the command of Admiral Rozhestvensky and would hopefully change the course of the war. The admiral, before they even set sail, said some of his subordinate senior officers were "manure sacks" and "vast empty space". He also had to deal with rebellious captains and other senior officers.

Although on paper sending reinforcements seemed like a good idea sending ships from the Baltic to the Pacific on a 10,000 mile journey when the ships couldn't hold supplies for half that distance, not to mention the fact that Russia had no bases in Africa or South-East Asia to resupply at which would make the this voyage into the literal embodiment of the phrase Voyage of the Dammed a voyage which would literally drive some of the men on it insane.
